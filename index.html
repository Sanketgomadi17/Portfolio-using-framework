The project involves designing and implementing a CNC (Computer Numerical Control) welding machine. This automation technology offers increased efficiency and precision in welding processes, reducing the need for human involvement in dangerous environments. CNC machines are known for their repeatability and accuracy, making them suitable for various applications, from large industries to small-scale projects and even household use. By integrating computer-aided design (CAD) and computer-aided manufacturing (CAM) programs, the machine can follow precise welding patterns and reduce welding time. It also enhances workplace safety by minimizing the risks associated with manual welding. The initial investment may be significant, but the long-term cost savings, scalability, and reduced skill requirements make CNC welding machines a valuable solution for improving welding efficiency and quality across different industries.


An obstacle avoiding robot is a fully autonomous robot which can be able to avoid any obstacle which it face when it move. Simply, when it met an obstacle while it moving forward, automatically stop moving forward and makes a step back. Then it looks it's two sides left & right and starts to move the best possible way; which means either in left direction if there is another obstacle in right or in right direction if there is another obstacle in left side. The obstacle avoiding robot is very helpful and it is the base of many large projects such as Automatic cars, robots used in Manufacturing factories, even in robots used in spacecrafts.
